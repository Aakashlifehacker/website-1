What happened in the world of Open Access this week?

Well, the obvious and most exciting event is clearly that the Open Access Button launched a new version! There have been pieces on us in the Huffington Post , Creative Commons , Open Knowledge Greece, Imperial College blogs , Electronics Frontier Foundation, Open Knowledge Foundation Blog , Open Education Europa and Venturebeat. Other highlights have included our launch, which is available to view online , taking part in a reddit AMA alongside EFF, Creative Commons, the Right to Research Coalition and Fundacion Karisma.

And in the wider world of Open Access...

LSE Library supported Open Access week.
Biomed central interviewed a group of students about Open Access
Jill Walker Rettberg wrote on how she published her book with a CC-BY license.
JISC launched their Open Access good practice project.
Right to Research Coalition wrote on how students and early career researchers are furthering Open Access.
The Open Knowledge Foundation have launched a new initiative on the future of Open Access in the humanities and social sciences.
BioMed Central have written on the state of Open Access in their journals compared to ten years ago.
Nature reported that for the period 2007-2012 over half of research published is now free to download somewhere online.
Theres a new Open Access palaeontology journal!
Nature communications is going fully Open Access .
The University of Southampton Library published a trio of blog posts for Open Access Week, you can read them here.

And if thats not enough for you, Right to Research Coalition have curated an impressive array of links to peruse at your leisure.
