We believe that researchers have an obligation to make research papers and the data supporting papers open by default. Open research and data is good practice for research, makes greater innovation possible, and is fair for funders - the public. Request pages keep researchers accountable to requests for papers and data made through the Open Access Button.

Open Data is data that is freely available on the internet permitting any user to download, copy, analyse, re-process, pass through software or use for any other purpose without financial, legal, or technical barriers. Open Data is great for research, because it accelerates discovery, increases integrity, and promotes spontaneity.

(*Occasionally, data can't be made open for privacy and other good reasons, which we understand. However, "It's hard" or "I don't have the time" are not good reasons.)

It's beneficial for researchers, too. Papers with publicly available data are cited more and the datasets themselves can be cited - allowing researchers to get greater credit for all their work. With Open Research Data bigger problems are solved faster. Better than data just sitting on your hard drive gathering dust, right?
