About

The Open Access Button is an app that helps researchers, patients, students and the public get legal access to research they need and request research be made available.

It all started in 2013 when two students, Joe McArthur and David Carroll, were tired of hitting paywalls. Instead of accepting their frustration, they became inspired and took an idea to track the impact of paywalls to a hackday. Together with volunteer developers the beta version of the Open Access Button was launched in November 2013. We've not stopped working since to connect people with the research they need and advocate for Open Access.

How it works

Finding free, legal, full-text articles

Before we create a request to the author we check that an article is not already legally available somewhere else. Our sources include all of the aggregated repositories in the world.

  • oaDOI
  • Share
  • CORE
  • OpenAIRE
  • Dissem.in
  • Europe PMC
  • BASE
    • Our goal is to link you directly to full text. We're getting better but still make mistakes. If you find one, report it here.

    Making more articles and data availiable

    When an article or dataset isn’t available we ask the authors to share one. We help authors share fast, legally and widely so that not only you get access - but everyone does, forever! The end result, no more paywalls and the data you need at your fingertips.

    Requests made through our system are public so others can join your request to motivate authors and we can hold them accountable. We believe authors should share, whenever possible. Research is publicly funded, and sharing is at the heart of the scientific method.

    We put articles and data in Zenodo and the Open Science Framework respectively for safe keeping.
