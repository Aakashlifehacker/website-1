Instructions

 

About the task:

In academic publishing, a paper is an academic work published in an academic journal containing original research results or reviewing existing results. Sometimes these papers are paywalled making that readers pay in order to read it. Some people cannot afford to pay for papers, and that’s where our software becomes handy.

 

Our software helps users get in contact with researchers and ask them to share their research articles and data. In order to do so, we need to find out the researcher’s email address.

 

Your primary task is to follow the instructions below and find the email address for the author’s of the articles given to you. While you do that, we ask that you pick up other information we need too!

 

  1. Look at article, see if an email address for the “corresponding author” is present.

  2. Copy the article title into google scholar.

    1. If a verified account for any author found (authors with verified accounts have their name highlighted and clickable), click it

      1. see if their email is present.

        1. Copy author name into Google Scholar. Look at other articles they’ve published and see if the email address is present. Look at the most recent articles first. If none found, go to 3.

    2. If verified account not found

      1. Copy author name into Google Scholar. Look at other articles they’ve published and see if the email address is present. Look at the most recent articles first. If none found, go to 3.

  3. Do a normal Google search for author name + institution (as seen on most recent paper or Google Scholar profile). The author’s email will often show on pages in the results.

  4. If all fails, repeat with other authors or give up. Use the name closest to the corresponding author.

  5. If you can't find the email address, leave the email field blank.
